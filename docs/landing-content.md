## The stakes (Amazon — the villain, instant context)

> In 2017, Amazon scrapped an AI recruiting tool. Trained to match candidates to past hires — who were mostly men — it taught itself to penalize the word *"women's"* and downgrade two all-women's colleges. It failed at exactly one task: screening résumés. That's the task this tool audits.

## What you're looking at (DATA DISCLOSURE — front and center, user-requested)

Everything here is **synthetic**. The résumés, candidates, and job reqs were generated — never scraped, never real people. Names and demographics are drawn from **US Census** and **SSA** data and the methodology of the landmark **Bertrand–Mullainathan** hiring-bias study, so the fairness tests are rigorous without putting one real person's data at risk. *A bias-audit tool that mishandled real résumés would defeat its own purpose.*

## What it does (three layers, plain language)

- **Screen** — a stand-in AI screener ranks candidates for a role, the way tools already on the market do.
- **Calibrate** — recruiters vote blind, head-to-head; we measure whether the AI agrees with them (Cohen's κ).
- **Audit** — we swap a candidate's name or gender while holding skills fixed, and check the four-fifths rule the way NYC **Local Law 144** requires.

You'll reproduce Amazon's failure in about a minute — then watch it get caught.
