AI Resume Screening Audit

Does your AI screener
pick resumes fairly?

HR Fidelity runs three independent checks — disparate impact, counterfactual drift, and recruiter-AI agreement — to certify or block your screener before it touches a single live application.

Why this matters: In 2018 Amazon discovered its ML hiring tool had learned to penalize résumés containing the word "women's" and downgraded graduates of all-women's colleges. The system was trained on ten years of hiring data — data that reflected a historically male engineering workforce. It was shut down before deployment, but the pattern repeats industry-wide.

HR Fidelity audits screeners before data like that can take hold. Every metric is computed on synthetic, public-domain data — no real candidates, no employer artifacts.

01 How it works
⚖️

Four-fifths disparate impact

EEOC / NYC Local Law 144 standard: any demographic group selected at less than 80 % of the top group's rate triggers a violation.

🔀

Counterfactual drift

Each résumé is paired with an identical twin where only one proxy signal changes (name, institution tier). Mean score drift > 5 % blocks certification.

📏

Recruiter–AI agreement (κ)

Cohen's κ measures whether the AI and human recruiters agree on A/B pairs. κ ≥ 0.60 ("substantial") is required to pass fidelity calibration.

🔒
Data disclosure — All résumés are 100 % synthetic. Names are sampled from SSA/Census/Bertrand-Mullainathan public datasets. No real candidate data, no employer hiring records. Every metric is recomputable from the open-source corpus generator.
